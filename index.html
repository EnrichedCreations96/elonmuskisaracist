Documented: Elon Musk's Racial Controversies

By Investigative Staff | April 5, 2025

On January 20, 2025, Elon Musk made a controversial gesture during Donald Trump's inauguration that sparked global debate. As documented on video and reported widely, Musk placed his hand on his chest and then extended his arm outward while saying "My heart goes out to you."

Elon Musk performing a controversial gesture at Trump's inauguration celebration, January 20, 2025.

This incident has renewed interest in examining Musk's documented history of racial controversies, particularly the multiple racial discrimination lawsuits against Tesla and his recent comments about South Africa.

Tesla Racial Discrimination Lawsuits

California DFEH v. Tesla (2022)

In February 2022, California's Department of Fair Employment and Housing filed a lawsuit against Tesla after a three-year investigation into conditions at the company's Fremont factory.

"After receiving hundreds of complaints from workers, DFEH found evidence that Tesla operates a racially segregated workplace where Black workers are subjected to racial slurs and discriminated against in job assignments, discipline, pay, and promotion."

Source: California Department of Fair Employment and Housing lawsuit filed February 2022 as reported by Newsweek on February 14, 2022.

According to the official lawsuit, Black workers at Tesla were subjected to racial slurs, with some reporting hearing such slurs "as often as 50-100 times a day." The lawsuit further claimed that some areas of the factory were described as "the plantation" or "the slave ship" due to racial segregation.

Source: NPR, "California sues Tesla over alleged rampant discrimination against Black employees," February 11, 2022.

Read the Newsweek report on the lawsuit

Owen Diaz v. Tesla

Former elevator operator Owen Diaz sued Tesla for racial discrimination in 2017. In April 2023, a federal jury awarded him $3.2 million in damages, and the case was settled in March 2024.

"The evidence was disturbing. The jury heard that the Tesla factory was saturated with racism."

Source: U.S. District Judge William Orrick's written order as reported by NPR on April 14, 2022.

Diaz testified that during his employment at Tesla's Fremont factory, he was subjected to racial slurs and other forms of racial harassment. The case initially resulted in a $137 million verdict that was later reduced by the judge before a new trial on damages.

Source: CNBC, "Tesla settles racial discrimination lawsuit," March 15, 2024.

Read the CNBC report on the settlement

Marcus Vaughn v. Tesla (Class Action)

In 2017, Tesla worker Marcus Vaughn filed a lawsuit that has since gathered testimonies from over 240 Black workers alleging racial discrimination at Tesla's Fremont factory. In March 2024, a judge ruled that thousands of Black workers can sue Tesla for racism.

"Tesla may face a class-action lawsuit after 240 Black factory workers in California described rampant racism and discrimination."

Source: Associated Press, "Black workers at California Tesla factory allege rampant racism, seek class-action status," June 7, 2023.

According to court documents, Vaughn complained in writing to human resources and to Tesla CEO Elon Musk about a hostile work environment in which he was called slurs by co-workers and supervisors. The lawsuit claims that no investigation was conducted and Vaughn was fired for "not having a positive attitude."

Source: TIME, "Thousands of Black Workers Can Sue Tesla for Racism, Judge Rules," March 1, 2024.

Read the TIME report on the class action

Musk's Comments on South Africa

Elon Musk was born and raised in South Africa during the apartheid era, leaving the country when he was 17. In recent months, he has made several documented comments about South Africa that have drawn attention:

"Racist Ownership Laws" Statement

In February 2025, Musk described South Africa as having "racist ownership laws" after his Starlink satellite internet service was denied a license for not meeting South Africa's affirmative action criteria.

Source: Washington Post, "Why is Trump punishing South Africa and who are the Afrikaners he wants to give refugee status to?" February 8, 2025.

"Musk also accused South Africa this week of having 'racist ownership laws,' an apparent reference to his failure to get a license in the country for his Starlink satellite internet service because it doesn't meet affirmative action criteria."
Source: Washington Post

White Farmer "Genocide" Claims

Musk has claimed that South Africa's government is ignoring or encouraging a "genocide" with regard to the killings of white farmers.

Source: NBC News, "How a land law sparked Elon Musk's accusations of 'genocide' against his home country," February 11, 2025.

"Musk has described South Africa as having 'racist ownership laws,' accusing its government of failing to stop what he has referred to as a 'genocide' against white farmers."
Source: NBC News
February 4, 2025

Musk's Comments on South Africa

Following Musk's statements on South Africa, Trump threatened to cut off funding to the country over what he called discrimination against white farmers.

Source: PBS NewsHour, "After Trump's funding threat, South African president phones influential billionaire Musk," February 6, 2025.

February 7, 2025

Trump Cuts Aid to South Africa

President Trump signed an executive order freezing assistance to South Africa for a law aiming to address some of the wrongs of South Africa's apartheid era.

Source: PBS NewsHour, "Trump orders freeze of aid to South Africa over post-apartheid land expropriation law," February 8, 2025.

Other Related Controversies

Tesla's Response to DFEH Lawsuit

When California's civil rights agency filed its lawsuit in 2022, Tesla responded with a blog post titled "The DFEH's Misguided Lawsuit," calling the lawsuit "unfair and counterproductive."

Source: NPR, "California sues Tesla over alleged rampant discrimination against Black employees," February 11, 2022.

"On the day the lawsuit was filed, Tesla issued a public blog post, titled 'The DFEH's Misguided Lawsuit,' where it called the lawsuit 'unfair and counterproductive.'"
Source: NPR

"Thick-Skinned" Email to Employees

A 2018 email from Musk to Tesla employees referenced in legal documents advised workers to be "thick-skinned" about harassment.

Source: Newsweek, "Tesla Faces California's Largest-Ever Racial Discrimination Lawsuit," February 14, 2022.

"Musk added, 'if someone is a jerk to you, but sincerely apologizes, it is important to be thick-skinned and accept that apology.'"
Source: Newsweek
